Frequently Asked Questions

Can't WordPress already schedule posts?

WordPress can schedule posts to publish at a future date. That's it. PostFlow lets you schedule any status change (publish → draft, draft → pending, etc.), content updates, category moves, and more. It's the scheduling WordPress should have built in.

How reliable is the scheduling?

PostFlow uses WordPress's built-in cron system (wp-cron). For sites with regular traffic, this works perfectly. For maximum reliability, we recommend setting up a real server cron job — PostFlow's documentation shows you how in 2 minutes.

Can I schedule bulk changes?

With Pro, yes. Select multiple posts and schedule the same change for all of them at once. Great for seasonal content, promotions, or editorial calendar management.

What if I need to cancel a scheduled change?

Easy — just delete the scheduled event from PostFlow's dashboard. You can also edit any scheduled change before it executes.

Will it slow down my site?

No. PostFlow is extremely lightweight. Scheduled checks happen via wp-cron and add negligible overhead. Your frontend is completely unaffected.

Is my data safe?

PostFlow stores everything in your WordPress database. No external services, no cloud, no tracking. Your content and schedule data never leave your server.
